# Macro Practitioner Framework

Use this skill when the user wants:

- A macro investing framework based on real-world practitioners rather than textbook finance
- Analysis of the current world, a country, an asset, or a macro theme
- Actionable guidance on what to watch, what matters, and how to express a view
- A repeatable checklist for macro monitoring and decision-making

## Core Worldview

Treat markets as adaptive systems, not equilibrium machines.

Use three practitioner lenses:

1. **Soros lens**
Prices, narratives, financing conditions, and policy responses can reshape fundamentals. Look for reflexive feedback loops.

2. **Dalio lens**
Debt, money, credit, cash flow, and policy constraints drive regime behavior. Identify the cycle and the unsustainable point.

3. **Druckenmiller lens**
Large gains come from a few windows when the evidence lines up. Use market internals and forward-looking signals, not only lagging macro data.

## Default Workflow

When using this skill, structure the analysis in this order:

1. Define the current macro regime in one sentence.
2. Identify the six main drivers:
   - growth
   - inflation
   - credit
   - liquidity
   - policy constraints
   - capital flows
3. Write the dominant causal chain.
4. Identify the reflexive feedback loop.
5. Identify the unsustainable point.
6. State the market consensus and likely mispricing.
7. List catalysts that could force repricing.
8. Choose the best asset expressions.
9. Define validation signals.
10. Define invalidation conditions.
11. Give positioning guidance scaled to uncertainty.

### Dominant Causal Chain

### Reflexive Loop
Explain how prices, policy, financing, or narratives may reinforce the move.

### Unsustainable Point
State what cannot keep going forever and what would break first.

### Market Mispricing
State what the market may still be underestimating or overestimating.

### Best Expressions
List the cleanest assets, styles, or cross-asset expressions.

### Validation Signals
List the few signals that would confirm the thesis.

### Invalidation Conditions
List the few signals that would break the thesis.

### Action Guidance
Convert the view into practical advice:
- what to lean into
- what to avoid
- what to monitor next

## Style Rules

- Avoid heavy academic jargon and equilibrium-model language.
- Prefer causal reasoning over factor laundry lists.
- Do not pretend to have precision when uncertainty is high.
- Separate the base case from monitoring signals.
- Keep recommendations conditional and scenario-aware.
- If using current facts, verify time-sensitive claims with up-to-date sources.

## Risk Discipline

Every analysis must include:

- what would make the view wrong
- what should happen in markets if the view is right
- what not to do too early

If conviction is low, say so and bias toward watchlists and conditional playbooks rather than bold positioning.

## Reusable Prompts

For a country or region:
"Analyze the current macro regime for [country/region] using the macro-practitioner-framework. Focus on drivers, reflexive loops, unsustainable points, market mispricing, and practical positioning guidance."

For a cross-asset setup:
"Use the macro-practitioner-framework to analyze [theme]. Tell me the regime, dominant causal chain, best asset expressions, validation signals, invalidation conditions, and what to do now."

For an update:
"Refresh the prior view using the macro-practitioner-framework. Highlight what changed in the driver map, what the market is pricing now, and what actions should change."
