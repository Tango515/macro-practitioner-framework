# Macro Practitioner Framework Reference

This reference summarizes the full framework in `macro_investing_framework.md`.

## One-Line Summary

Macro investing is the practice of identifying how the world is moving away from an old equilibrium, how that move is being reinforced by policy, credit, liquidity, and behavior, and where the resulting repricing can be expressed with good odds and manageable risk.

## Six Drivers

1. Growth
2. Inflation
3. Credit
4. Liquidity
5. Policy constraints
6. Capital flows

## Analysis Checklist

For every analysis, answer:

1. What regime are we in?
2. What is the main causal chain?
3. What reflexive loop is operating?
4. What is unsustainable?
5. What is the market consensus?
6. What is likely mispriced?
7. What catalysts matter next?
8. What are the cleanest asset expressions?
9. What validates the thesis?
10. What invalidates the thesis?

## Positioning Discipline

- Wait for enough alignment before sizing up.
- Avoid building size on pure imagination.
- Do not wait for every lagging data point to confirm.
- If markets do not respond as expected, reduce and reassess.
- Big gains usually come from a few high-conviction windows, not constant activity.
